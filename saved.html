A 65-year-old male with chronic kidney disease (Stage 3) requires initiation of direct oral anticoagulant (DOAC) therapy for non-valvular atrial fibrillation. Which agent requires the most significant dose adjustment?

Your Answer (Incorrect)

Apixaban

Correct Answer

Dabigatran

Clinical Rationale

Dabigatran is predominantly cleared by the kidneys (~80%). In patients with CrCl 15-30 mL/min, the dosage must be reduced to 75 mg twice daily. Apixaban has significantly less renal clearance (~27%) and primarily requires dose adjustment based on a combination of age, body weight, and serum creatinine, not solely CrCl.

edit_note
edit Personal Note

"Always check the 80% rule for Dabigatran. Apixaban relies on the ABC criteria (Age >80, Body weight <60kg, Creatinine >1.5). Don't mix up the clearance pathways during exams."

Evaluate the interaction between Linezolid and Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs). What is the primary mechanistic concern?

Correct Answer

Serotonin Syndrome due to non-selective MAO inhibition by Linezolid.

Clinical Rationale

Linezolid possesses weak, reversible, nonselective monoamine oxidase (MAO) inhibitory activity. Concurrent use with serotonergic agents like SSRIs increases the risk of serotonin syndrome, characterized by altered mental status, autonomic instability, and neuromuscular abnormalities.
