Question 14 of 50 High Yield

A 45-year-old male presents to the emergency department with severe, tearing chest pain radiating to his back. His blood pressure is 180/110 mmHg. An ECG shows sinus tachycardia with non-specific ST-T wave changes. A chest X-ray reveals a widened mediastinum. Which of the following is the most appropriate initial intravenous medication to administer?

school

Clinical Rationale

The patient's presentation (tearing chest pain, hypertension, widened mediastinum) is highly suggestive of an acute aortic dissection.

The initial goal of medical therapy is to reduce aortic wall shear stress (dP/dt). This is achieved by first administering an intravenous beta-blocker (such as Esmolol) to decrease heart rate and inotropy.

Crucial Concept: Vasodilators (like sodium nitroprusside) should NEVER be initiated before beta-blockade in aortic dissection, as they can cause reflex tachycardia, thereby increasing dP/dt and worsening the dissection.
