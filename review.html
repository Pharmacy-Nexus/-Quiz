Apixaban 5 mg twice daily Correct Answer

science Clinical Rationale

In patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation and a CHA2DS2-VASc score of ≥2 in men (or ≥3 in women), oral anticoagulation is recommended to reduce the risk of stroke. Direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs), such as apixaban, are generally preferred over warfarin due to a lower risk of major bleeding (particularly intracranial hemorrhage) and lack of routine monitoring requirements. Aspirin monotherapy is inadequate for stroke prevention in patients with this risk profile and is no longer recommended.
